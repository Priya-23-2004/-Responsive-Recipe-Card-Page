Poori
$50

Description:
A puffed, deep-fried Indian bread made from whole wheat flour. Crisp outside, soft inside—perfect with curries or halwa.
Ingredients:
2 cups whole wheat flour ½ tsp salt 1 tbsp semolina (optional) 1 tbsp oil (for dough) ¾ cup water (as needed) Oil for deep frying
Preparation Instructions:
Mix flour, salt, semolina, and oil. Add water gradually and knead into a firm dough. Rest dough for 15–20 mins. Divide into small balls. Roll into 4–5 inch circles. Heat oil; fry one poori at a time. Press gently to puff, flip, and fry until golden. Drain on paper towel. Serve hot.

Porotta
$100

Description:
A flaky, layered South Indian flatbread made with all-purpose flour. Crispy outside, soft and chewy inside—perfect with spicy curries.
Ingredients:
2 cups all-purpose flour (maida) 1 tsp sugar 1 tsp salt 2 tbsp oil or ghee ¾ cup water (as needed) More oil for cooking and layering
Preparation Instructions:
Mix flour, sugar, salt, and oil. Add water gradually; knead into a soft, smooth dough. Rest for 1–2 hours, covered. Divide into small balls; coat with oil and rest 10 mins. Roll each ball very thin, stretch into a sheet. Fold like a fan or coil into a spiral; flatten gently. Roll out again into a thick disc. Cook on a hot pan with oil till golden on both sides. Stack and gently clap (or crush) to release layers. Serve hot with curry or chutney.

Chicken Briyani
$150

Description:
A fragrant and flavorful rice dish made with marinated chicken, basmati rice, and aromatic spices. A festive favorite across India.
Ingredients:
2 cups basmati rice 500g chicken (with bone, cut into pieces) 1 cup yogurt 2 onions (sliced) 2 tomatoes (chopped) 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste ½ tsp turmeric 1 tsp red chili powder 1½ tsp garam masala Whole spices: 4 cloves, 2 cardamoms, 1 bay leaf, 1 cinnamon stick Fresh mint and coriander leaves 4 tbsp oil or ghee Salt to taste Water as needed Saffron or yellow food color (optional)
Preparation Instructions:
Marinate chicken with yogurt, turmeric, chili powder, salt, and ginger-garlic paste. Rest 30 mins. Cook rice 70% done with whole spices. Drain and set aside. Fry onions till golden brown; remove half for garnishing. Add tomatoes and cook till soft. Add marinated chicken, mint, coriander, and garam masala. Cook till chicken is 80% done. Layer rice over chicken. Add fried onions, more mint/coriander, and saffron water (if using). Cover tightly and cook on low (dum) for 20–25 mins. Fluff and serve hot with raita or salan.

Banana Leaf Meal
$200

Description:
A rich and colorful South Indian vegetarian meal with a variety of sweet, sour, and spicy dishes served on a banana leaf. It includes rice, curries, chutneys, pickles, desserts, and more.
Typical Ingredients (Dish Components):
Parboiled rice (with ghee) Sambar – Lentil curry with vegetables Rasam – Spicy tamarind-based soup Parippu – Moong dal curry with ghee Avial – Mixed vegetables with coconut and yogurt Thoran – Stir-fried cabbage or beans with coconut Olan – Ash gourd in coconut milk Kaalan – Yogurt curry with yam or raw banana Pachadi – Yogurt-based chutney (often beetroot) Kichadi – Yogurt curry with cucumber or bitter gourd Erissery – Pumpkin and black-eyed peas curry Koottu Curry – Black chana with roasted coconut Pickles – Lemon, mango, or ginger Banana chips and Sharkara upperi (jaggery chips) Papadam – Fried crispy wafer Curd – Plain yogurt or spiced buttermilk Payasam – Dessert (semolina or lentil pudding) Banana – Served at the start of the meal Salt
Basic Serving Instructions:
Lay a fresh banana leaf with the tip pointing left. Serve dry items on the top half of the leaf. Place rice at the center-bottom with a spoon of ghee. Start with parippu, then sambar, followed by rasam. End with curd and payasam. Eat with hands, starting from mild to spicy dishes.

Pizza
$250

Description:
A classic Italian flatbread topped with sauce, cheese, and your favorite toppings. Crispy crust, melty cheese—perfect for any occasion.
Ingredients:
2 cups all-purpose flour 1 tsp dry yeast 1 tsp sugar ½ tsp salt ¾ cup warm water 1 tbsp olive oil ½ cup pizza sauce 1 cup mozzarella cheese (grated) Toppings: onion, capsicum, olives, pepperoni (as desired)
Preparation Instructions:
Mix yeast, sugar, and warm water. Let sit 10 mins. Add flour, salt, and oil. Knead into soft dough. Cover and rest for 1 hour till doubled. Preheat oven to 220°C (430°F). Roll dough into a circle or rectangle. Spread sauce, sprinkle cheese, add toppings. Bake for 12–15 mins or until crust is golden. Slice and serve hot!

Dosa
$25

Description:
A crispy South Indian crepe made from fermented rice and lentil batter, filled with spiced potato masala. Served hot with chutneys and sambar.
Ingredients:
For Dosa Batter: 2 cups rice ½ cup urad dal (split black gram) 2 tbsp poha (flattened rice) Salt to taste Water, as needed For Potato Masala: 3 boiled potatoes (mashed) 1 onion (sliced) 1 green chili (chopped) ½ tsp mustard seeds 1 tsp chana dal ½ tsp turmeric A few curry leaves Salt, oil
Preparation Instructions:
Soak rice, dal, and poha for 4–6 hrs. Grind to a smooth batter, add salt, and ferment overnight. Heat oil, add mustard seeds, chana dal, curry leaves. Sauté onions, green chili, turmeric. Add mashed potatoes and salt. Mix well. Heat a dosa pan, pour batter and spread thin. Drizzle oil, cook till crisp. Place potato masala in center. Fold and serve with coconut chutney and sambar.

Idli
$25

Description:
A soft, fluffy South Indian breakfast dish made from fermented rice and urad dal batter. Light, healthy, and perfect with chutney and sambar.
Ingredients:
2 cups rice 1 cup urad dal (split black gram) 2 tbsp poha (optional, for softness) Salt to taste Water, as needed
Preparation Instructions:
Soak rice, urad dal, and poha separately for 4–6 hours. Grind dal to a smooth batter. Grind rice and poha to a slightly coarse batter. Mix both batters, add salt, and let ferment overnight. Grease idli molds and pour batter. Steam for 10–12 mins or until a toothpick comes out clean. Cool slightly, then remove and serve hot with chutney and sambar.

Ven Pongal (South Indian Ghee Rice)
$125

Description:
A soft and flavorful rice dish made with moong dal, ghee, black pepper, and cashews. Commonly eaten for breakfast with sambar and chutney.
Ingredients:
1 cup raw rice ½ cup moong dal 2 tbsp ghee 1 tsp black pepper (crushed or whole) 1 tsp cumin seeds 10 cashews 1 inch ginger (chopped) Few curry leaves ½ tsp salt (or to taste) 4 cups water
Preparation Instructions:
Dry roast moong dal till light golden. Wash rice and dal, cook together in 4 cups water until soft. Mash lightly when done. In a pan, heat ghee, add cumin, pepper, ginger, cashews, curry leaves. Fry till golden, then pour into cooked pongal. Mix well, adjust salt. Serve hot with sambar and coconut chutney.

$15

Description:
Crispy, golden-fried potato balls flavored with spices and herbs, coated in besan (gram flour) batter. A perfect tea-time snack!
Ingredients:
For Potato Filling: 3 boiled potatoes (mashed) 1 green chili (chopped) 1 inch ginger (grated) ½ tsp mustard seeds A pinch of turmeric Few curry leaves Salt to taste 1 tbsp oil For Batter: 1 cup gram flour (besan) ¼ tsp turmeric A pinch of baking soda (optional) Salt to taste Water (to make thick batter) Other: Oil for deep frying
Preparation Instructions:
Heat oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, green chili, and ginger. Add turmeric, mashed potatoes, and salt. Mix well. Cool and make small balls. In a bowl, mix besan, turmeric, salt, and water into a thick batter. Dip potato balls in batter and deep fry until golden. Serve hot with tomato ketchup or chutney.

Medu Vada (Urad Dal Fritters)
$10

Description:
A crunchy, savory donut-shaped fritter made from urad dal batter, flavored with spices. Often eaten with coconut chutney and sambar for breakfast or snacks.
Ingredients:
1 cup urad dal (split black gram) 1–2 green chilies (chopped) 1 inch ginger (grated) Few curry leaves (chopped) 1 onion (finely chopped, optional) Salt to taste Oil for deep frying
Preparation Instructions:
Soak urad dal for 4–5 hours, then drain. Grind into a thick, fluffy batter (no or little water). Add chili, ginger, curry leaves, onion, and salt. Mix well. Wet your hands, shape batter into rings with a hole in the center. Deep fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy. Serve hot with sambar, coconut chutney, and tomato chutney.

Gulab Jamun
$25

Description:
Soft, juicy, and melt-in-the-mouth sweet dumplings made with khoya or milk powder, deep-fried and soaked in fragrant sugar syrup.
Ingredients:
1 cup khoya (or milk powder) 2 tbsp maida (all-purpose flour) 1/4 tsp baking soda 2–3 tbsp milk (for kneading) Ghee or oil (for frying) For sugar syrup: 1.5 cups sugar 1.5 cups water A few drops of rose water or cardamom pods
Preparation Instructions:
Mix khoya, flour & soda to form a smooth dough with milk. Shape into small balls. Heat oil/ghee & deep-fry balls on low flame till golden brown. In another pan, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar and water. Add rose water/cardamom. Soak fried balls in warm syrup for at least 1 hour. Serve warm or chilled.
